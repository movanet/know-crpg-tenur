# South Africa (19 definitions)

[Back to Index](.)



### Aquifer


a geological formation which has structures or textures that hold water or permit appreciable water movement through them


  - **National Water Act 36 of 1998, s.1, (1998) [TIER 2]**


---


### catchment


in relation to a watercourse or watercourses or part of a watercourse, the area from which any rainfall will drain into the watercourse or watercourses or part of a watercourse, through surface flow to a common point or common points


  - **National Water Act 36 of 1998, s.1, (1998) [TIER 2]**


---


### charge


includes a fee, price or tariff imposed under this Act


  - **National Water Act 36 of 1998, s.1, (1998) [TIER 2]**


---


### conservation


efficient water use achieved through saving devices, efficient processes, demand management, and rationing measures


  - **National Water Act 36 of 1998, s.1, (1998) [TIER 2]**


---


### consumer


any person receiving water services or who is entitled to receive water services from a water services institution


  - **Water Services Act 108 of 1997, s.1, (1997) [TIER 2]**


---


### entitlement


a right to use water under any provision of the Act or an instrument issued under it


  - **National Water Act 36 of 1998, s.1, (1998) [TIER 2]**


---


### estuary


a partially or fully enclosed body of water which is open to the sea permanently or periodically, where seawater can be diluted with fresh water from land


  - **National Water Act 36 of 1998, s.1, (1998) [TIER 2]**


---


### person


includes a natural person, a juristic person, an unincorporated body, an association, an organ of state and the Minister


  - **National Water Act 36 of 1998 (South Africa), s.1 [TIER 2]**


---


### pollution


the direct or indirect alteration of the physical, chemical or biological properties of a water resource so as to make it less fit for any beneficial purpose for which it may reasonably be expected to be used


  - **National Water Act 36 of 1998, s.1, (1998) [TIER 2]**


---


### right of access to basic water supply and basic sanitation





  - **South Africa, Water Services Act 1997 (Act No. 108 of 1997) [TIER 2]**


---


### sanitation services


the collection, removal, disposal or purification of human excreta, domestic wastewater, sewage and effluent resulting from the use of water for commercial purposes


  - **Water Services Act 108 of 1997, s.1, (1997) [TIER 2]**


---


### waste


any solid material or material that is suspended, dissolved or transported in water (including sediment) and which is spilled or deposited on land or into a water resource in such volume, composition or manner as to cause, or to be reasonably likely to cause, the water resource to be polluted


  - **National Water Act 36 of 1998, s.1, (1998) [TIER 2]**


---


### water management area


an area established as a management unit in the national water resource strategy within which a catchment management agency will conduct the protection, use, development, conservation, management and control of water resources


  - **National Water Act 36 of 1998 (South Africa), s.1 [TIER 2]**


---


### water resource


includes a watercourse, surface water, estuary, or aquifer


  - **National Water Act 36 of 1998, s.1, (1998) [TIER 2]**


---


### water services


water supply services and sanitation services


  - **Water Services Act 108 of 1997, s.1, (1997) [TIER 2]**


---


### water services authority


a municipality with responsibility for providing water services to consumers within its jurisdiction


  - **Water Services Act 108 of 1997, s.1, (1997) [TIER 2]**


---


### water services institution


a water services authority, a water services provider, a water board and a water services committee


  - **Water Services Act 108 of 1997, s.1, (1997) [TIER 2]**


---


### water services provider


any person who provides water services to consumers or to another water services institution, but does not include a water services intermediary


  - **Water Services Act 108 of 1997, s.1, (1997) [TIER 2]**


---


### Watercourse


includes a river, stream, tributary or natural channel in which water flows regularly or intermittently


  - **National Water Act 36 of 1998, s.1, (1998) [TIER 2]**
